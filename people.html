OpenLexington's Civic Hackers

Chase Southard
President
twitter: @southard
email: chas...@gmail.com

R. Chase Southard (sounds like: Suh' thurd) is a Kentucky native and an alumnus of the University of Kentucky receiving his BS in Biological Sciences. A biologist seduced by computers is an apt description. Chase is a Research Analyst in the UK College of Medicine, Department of Molecular and Biomedical Pharmacology where he integrates biological and computer sciences while investigating the molecular biology of breast cancer. Inspired by the work of the Sunlight Foundation, Code for America, OpenPlans, and others, Chase with the help of some like-minded friends founded OpenLexington to find, build, and collaborate on open source solutions for open data and open local government in Lexington, Kentucky.

 

David Rhodus
VP & Communications Director
twitter: @davidrhodus

David Rhodus is a native of Richmond, Kentucky and moved to Lexington in 2006. He helped run one of the first internet service provider's in Kentucky during the 90's. He spent the next decade working in the health insurance industry as a systems programmer. David is currently an independent consultant.

 

Todd Willey
Government Liaison to LFUCG LeXray Committee
twitter: @xtoddx
email: xto...@gmail.com

Todd Willey is the epitome of geek. He grows beard and isn't afraid of anything. He is the founder of Collexion, a local hackerspace that encourages collaboration and experimentation. He currently spends most of his time building open source cloud computing infrastructure for NASA at ANSO Labs. He is a founding partner of Rubidine, a programming shop focusing on secure transaction processing for healthcare and solving other large scale integration problems. He is always up for biking or backpacking, just don't expect to get on the trail before noon.
